Company FAQ

What is Curefoods?
Curefoods is a Bengaluru-headquartered house of food brands, founded in 2020 by Ankit Nagori. It builds and scales multiple F&B brands — spanning healthy meals, biryani, pizza, desserts and celebration cakes — out of a shared network of cloud kitchens and dine-in stores across India.
How many brands does Curefoods own?
Curefoods' published brand portfolio includes Kitchens of EatFit, Sharief Bhai, Olio Pizza, Arambam, Nomad Pizza, CakeZone and Frozen Bottle, plus master-franchise partner Krispy Kreme.
What is a 'house of brands' model, and why does Curefoods use it?
A house-of-brands model runs several distinct, independently-branded products from shared infrastructure — in Curefoods' case, shared cloud kitchens, supply chain and delivery operations. It lets one kitchen serve several occasions (health food, biryani, dessert) to the same customer without diluting any single brand's identity.
How many kitchens and stores does Curefoods operate?
As of March 31, 2025, Curefoods runs 281 cloud kitchens, 99 kiosks and 122 restaurants across India.
Who founded Curefoods and when?
Curefoods was founded in 2020 by Ankit Nagori, former co-founder of Cure.fit (Cult.fit) and former Chief Business Officer of Flipkart, who set out to apply the same operating rigor to the food-brands business.
How can I order from Curefoods brands?
Every Curefoods brand is listed on major food delivery platforms (Swiggy, Zomato) under its own name — search the brand name directly, e.g. 'EatFit' or 'Sharief Bhai' — and select brands also operate dedicated apps and dine-in stores.
Is Curefoods the same company as Rebel Foods?
No. Curefoods and Rebel Foods are separate, competing Indian cloud-kitchen companies that both use a house-of-brands model. Rebel Foods owns brands like Faasos, Behrouz Biryani and Oven Story; Curefoods owns EatFit, Sharief Bhai, CakeZone and the brands listed on this site.
Does Curefoods have physical, dine-in stores or only delivery kitchens?
Both. Most Curefoods brands operate delivery-only cloud kitchens, but several — including Sharief Bhai's Mosque Road flagship and Krispy Kreme's dine-in cafés — also run dine-in and walk-in retail formats.
How do I apply for a job at Curefoods?
Open roles across all Curefoods brands are listed on the Careers page (curefoods.in/careers.html); applications can also be sent to careers@curefoods.in.
How can franchise or partnership enquiries reach Curefoods?
Franchise and partnership enquiries can be sent to hello@curefoods.in.

Brand FAQ

EatFit

What makes EatFit different from a regular food delivery brand?
Every EatFit dish is nutrition-labelled with exact calories, protein, carbs and fat, designed with dietitians so customers can hit fitness goals without home-cooking.
What is 'Kitchens of EatFit'?
Kitchens of EatFit is the shared-kitchen umbrella that houses EatFit alongside seven sister brands — HRX by EatFit, Great Indian Khichdi, Homeplate, Chaat Street, Rolls on Wheels, Millet Express and Madras Curd Rice Company — so one order can mix dishes from all of them.
Which cities is EatFit available in?
EatFit operates in Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Mumbai and Delhi NCR, with kitchens expanding as Curefoods opens new cloud-kitchen clusters.

Sharief Bhai

Where is Sharief Bhai's flagship store?
Sharief Bhai's flagship dine-in store is on Mosque Road in Frazer Town, Bengaluru — the city's historic biryani and old-city food street.
Is Sharief Bhai available outside Karnataka?
Sharief Bhai runs 54 stores across 10 cities in Karnataka (Bengaluru, Mysore, Mangalore, Manipal, Tumkur, Hassan), Tamil Nadu (Chennai, Coimbatore, Hosur) and Puducherry — it does not currently have stores in Hyderabad or the rest of Telangana.
What is the difference between Sharief Bhai and Roz Shawarma?
Roz Shawarma is Sharief Bhai's sibling brand focused only on shawarma and rolls, sharing kitchens but running a lighter, faster menu for quick-bite occasions.

Olio

What is Enso and how is it related to Olio?
Enso is a related, smaller-format concept run out of select Olio kitchens as Curefoods tests new menu formats; it is not available in every city Olio serves.

Arambam

What does '5 states in 1 plate' mean for Arambam?
It's Arambam's positioning line — a single menu spanning South Indian regional styles across five states, built around millets instead of rice or wheat.
Is Arambam the same company as Millet Express?
Arambam is run by Curefoods' Millet Express subsidiary, which also operates the separate Millet Express brand; the two share ownership but run distinct menus and storefronts.

Krispy Kreme

Is Krispy Kreme a Curefoods-owned brand or a franchise?
Krispy Kreme is a global brand; Curefoods operates it in India as master franchisee, running local stores and delivery under Krispy Kreme's international recipes and standards rather than owning the underlying IP.

Nomad Pizza

What makes Nomad Pizza different from other pizza delivery brands?
Nomad Pizza hand-stretches every base to order and builds its menu around globally-inspired toppings like Peri Peri and Korean BBQ alongside classics.

CakeZone

Does CakeZone deliver at midnight?
Yes — CakeZone offers midnight and same-day delivery slots designed for last-minute birthday and anniversary celebrations.

Curefoods is India's house of food brands, building and scaling category-defining F&B brands — from healthy meals to biryani, pizza to desserts — out of 281 cloud kitchens, 99 kiosks and 122 restaurants.
