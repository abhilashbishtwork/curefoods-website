About Curefoods

We build food brands the way operators build companies.

Founded in 2020 by Ankit Nagori, Curefoods is a Bengaluru, Karnataka, India-headquartered house of food brands running 15+ brands from a shared kitchen network across 60+ Indian cities.

Timeline

2020

Curefoods founded by Ankit Nagori; EatFit launches as the flagship brand.

2021

Portfolio expands rapidly — Sharief Bhai, Nomad Pizza, Olio, Frozen Bottle and CakeZone join or launch.

2022

Roz Shawarma, Rolls on Wheels and Chaat Street launch; Curefoods signs on as Krispy Kreme's India master franchisee; Kitchens of EatFit shared-kitchen model takes shape.

2023

'Kitchens of EatFit' formally launches with Hrithik Roshan as brand ambassador and investor; Millet Express and Madras Curd Rice Company join.

2024

Curefoods crosses ₹1,420 Cr in FY24 revenue, operating 400+ kitchens and stores across 60+ cities; Arambam launches with Rakul Preet Singh.

How we operate

What makes the model work

Kitchen-first economics

Every brand is designed around shared kitchen infrastructure, so unit economics work before marketing spend does.

Category depth over breadth

Each brand owns one occasion — health, biryani, dessert, celebration — rather than trying to be everything to everyone.

Data-run operations

Menu, pricing and kitchen placement decisions are driven by order-level data across every brand and city.

Speed to the door

Kitchen density and route optimisation are built to shrink delivery time without shrinking food quality.
