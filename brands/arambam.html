Arambam

5 states in 1 plate — wholesome South Indian, built on millets.

Arambam is Curefoods' celebrity-backed South Indian brand, run out of its Millet Express subsidiary, serving comforting millet-forward regional dishes from across five South Indian states on one menu.

The story

Arambam launched in partnership with actor Rakul Preet Singh, positioned around the nutritional case for millets over rice and wheat. Run by Curefoods' Millet Express subsidiary, it later entered an 18-month master franchise agreement with EAT360 to accelerate expansion beyond its Bengaluru and Mumbai launch markets.

FAQ

Questions about Arambam

What does '5 states in 1 plate' mean for Arambam?
It's Arambam's positioning line — a single menu spanning South Indian regional styles across five states, built around millets instead of rice or wheat.
Is Arambam the same company as Millet Express?
Arambam is run by Curefoods' Millet Express subsidiary, which also operates the separate Millet Express brand; the two share ownership but run distinct menus and storefronts.
